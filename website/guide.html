Setup Guide

Everything you need to get your K7 lamp under full control — no technical knowledge required.

What is this?

The K7 LED Controller is a small add-on device that gives your Noo-Psyche K7 Mini or K7 Pro aquarium light a proper web interface — accessible from any phone, tablet, or computer connected to the same WiFi.

Once set up it runs 24 hours a day, completely independently. Your phone or laptop is only needed to make changes — the controller keeps the lighting schedule running even when everything else is switched off.

What you need to buy

1

Seeed Studio XIAO ESP32-S3

A tiny WiFi-enabled microcontroller about the size of a postage stamp. Search for "XIAO ESP32-S3" on Amazon, AliExpress, Mouser, or Seeed Studio directly. Costs around £6–£10 / $8–$12.

Either the standard version or the "Sense" version will work. You do not need any other variant.

2

USB-C cable

Any USB-C cable works for both flashing (setting up) and for permanent power. You may already have one — a phone charging cable is fine.

3

USB phone charger

Any USB charger with a spare port. The board uses less power than a phone on standby, so any old charger is fine.

That's it. No soldering. No special cables to the lamp. The controller connects to your lamp over WiFi, so there are no wires between them.
1

Flash the firmware

"Flashing" means loading the K7 Controller software onto the board. You only need to do this once.

  1. a. Open Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, or Opera on a laptop or desktop computer. (Firefox and Safari will not work for this step.)
  2. b. Plug your XIAO ESP32-S3 into the computer using the USB-C cable.
  3. c. Go to the Flash Firmware page and click Install Firmware. Follow the on-screen prompts to select the device and confirm the install.
  4. d. Wait for the progress bar to finish. The board will reboot automatically.
If the device is not detected: Hold the small BOOT (B) button on the board while pressing the RST button, then release both. Click Install Firmware again. Also check that you are using a data-capable USB cable — some cables are charge-only and will not work.
2

Connect it to your lamp

After flashing, the board needs to know where your lamp is. Do this from your phone or the same computer — it takes about two minutes.

  1. a.
    Make sure your K7 lamp is switched on.
  2. b.
    On your phone or laptop, open the WiFi settings and connect to the network called K7-Setup. There is no password — just tap Connect.
  3. c.
    Open a web browser and go to http://192.168.5.1. You should see a setup page with a list of nearby lamps.
  4. d.
    Your lamp should appear in the list (it will have a name like K7mini_XXXX). Tap it to select it, then tap Connect & Save.
  5. e.
    The board reboots. The K7-Setup network will disappear — that is normal and means it worked.
Lamp not in the list? Make sure the lamp is powered on and within a few metres of the board. Tap Scan again to search once more. You can also type the lamp's WiFi name manually — it is printed on a sticker on the lamp, or visible in your phone's WiFi settings when the lamp is on.
3

Open the controller

  1. a.
    In your WiFi settings, connect to the network called K7-Controller. The password is 12345678.
  2. b.
    Open a browser and go to http://192.168.5.1. The controller will load and automatically read the current settings from your lamp.
  3. c.
    Unplug the USB-C cable from your computer and plug it into a USB charger near the tank. The controller is now running permanently — you don't need a computer connected any more.
Bookmark it. Add http://192.168.5.1 as a bookmark on your phone's home screen so you can open it with one tap whenever you're connected to K7-Controller. On iPhone: tap the Share button → Add to Home Screen. On Android: tap the menu → Add to Home Screen.

Using the controller

Setting the lighting schedule

The main screen shows a graph with 24 hours across the bottom and brightness on the side. Drag any point up or down to change the brightness for that hour. When you're happy, tap Push to send the schedule to the lamp.

Presets

Don't know where to start? Use the presets dropdown to load a ready-made schedule for Fish Only, LPS Reef, SPS Reef, or Mixed Reef. You can then adjust it to suit your tank and save it as your own named profile.

Smooth Ramp

By default the lamp steps between brightness levels once per hour, which looks a bit abrupt. Turn on Smooth Ramp and the controller will gradually adjust the brightness every minute so transitions look like a natural sunrise and sunset.

Lunar Cycle

Enables a gentle blue moonlight glow overnight that follows the real moon. On a full moon you'll see a soft blue shimmer; on a new moon it's completely off. The 29.5-day cycle runs automatically — just switch it on.

Lightning & Clouds

Optional effects for a more natural look. Lightning produces occasional white flashes — you can set a time window so it only fires in the evening. Clouds makes the light periodically dim for 20–90 seconds as if clouds were passing overhead, with the frequency controlled by the density slider.

Troubleshooting

The K7-Controller WiFi doesn't appear after setup
Unplug and re-plug the USB power on the board to restart it. Wait about 10 seconds then look for K7-Controller in your WiFi list again. If K7-Setup reappears instead, the lamp connection was not saved — go through Step 2 again.
The controller opens but shows an error reading from the lamp
Make sure the lamp is powered on. The K7 lamp creates its own WiFi network — if the lamp is off or in standby that network disappears and the controller cannot reach it. Switch the lamp back on and tap the refresh button.
I pushed a schedule but the lamp didn't change
Check that the lamp is in Auto mode (not Manual) using the mode toggle at the top of the controller page. Also make sure the schedule actually has non-zero values for the current hour of the day.
My phone lost internet when I connected to K7-Controller
That is expected — the K7-Controller network is only for talking to the lamp, not for general internet browsing. Switch back to your normal home WiFi when you're done making changes. The controller keeps running your schedule in the background regardless.
I want to change the WiFi password or lamp connection
Flash the firmware again (Step 1) and redo the WiFi setup (Step 2). Your saved profiles and schedules will be lost, so note them down first.
Need to reflash? Go to the Flash Firmware page.
